# Elias Turner

Key Life Events

- 1844 — Born in Knox County, Illinois to a family of subsistence farmers of Scots-Irish descent.
- 1862–1865 — Served in the Union Army with the 103rd Illinois Infantry during the Civil War; wounded at the Battle of Resaca (May 1864).
- 1867 — Married Margaret Hale in Peoria, Illinois after returning home and working as a millhand.
- 1869 — Claimed 160 acres under the Homestead Act near Osceola Township, Iowa, building a one-room cabin by hand.
- 1873 — Survived the “Grasshopper Plague” that devastated crops across the Midwest; rebuilt the farm with help from neighbors.
- 1878 — Completed construction of the main farmhouse and barn, expanding into wheat and hogs.
- 1880 — Sat for the family portrait that now represents the Turners’ early life on the prairie.
- 1895 — Elected to the township board, advocating for a permanent schoolhouse and improved rural roads.
- 1902 — Passed away on the farm, leaving the land to his eldest son.
