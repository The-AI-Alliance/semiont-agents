# Margaret Hale

- 1848 — Born in Peoria County, Illinois to Samuel and Ruth Hale, small-town shopkeepers.
- 1864 — Began work as a teacher’s assistant at the local schoolhouse during the latter years of the Civil War.
- 1867 — Married Elias Turner in Peoria, Illinois, after meeting him at a church social shortly after his return from service.
- 1869 — Relocated to Osceola Township, Iowa, where she and Elias claimed 160 acres under the Homestead Act.
- 1871 — Gave birth to her first child, Clara, in the original one-room cabin built on their claim.
- 1873 — Helped organize the township’s first women’s mutual-aid circle during the Grasshopper Plague, coordinating food sharing and childcare.
- 1878 — Oversaw the planting of a large kitchen garden and orchard after the main farmhouse was completed.
- 1880 — Appeared in the Turner family portrait, depicting the young family’s early farmstead life.
- 1896 — Became a founding member of the Osceola Women’s Temperance and Literacy Society, teaching reading to farmstead children during harvest off-seasons.
- 1919 — Passed away in the Turner farmhouse, surrounded by her children and grandchildren.
